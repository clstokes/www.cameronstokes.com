Tasting: Dogfish Head My Antonia

Nov 28th, 2010

My Antonia was born from a collaboration with Dogfish Head’s founder Sam Calgione and Leonardo DiVencenzo of the Italian brewery Birra del Borgo. My Antonia is an imperial pilsner brewed using Dogfish Head’s now famous continuous hopping process where hops are introduced throughout the boil rather than at set intervals. The first batch was brewed in Italy with a limited import to The States. In 2010, Dogfish Head brewed their inaugural batch at the Dogfish Head brewery in Delaware. My tasting is from the U.S. version.

Beer Facts

Name: My Antonia

Style: Imperial Pilsner

Brewery: Dogfish Head Craft Brewery, Inc.

Released: June 2010

Availability: “Occasional rarity” in 750 ml. bottles and draft.

Description:

This here beer is a lagerific departure from our ale-centric brewery. A marriage of old world tradition and new world innovation, My Antonia is brewed with pilsner malt and continually-hopped with a blend of Noble and West Coast hops.

Hops: “Noble and West Coast hops”

Malts: “Pilsner malt”

OG: ???

FG: ???

IBUs: ???

ABV: 7.5%

Tasting

Serving: From a 750 ml. bottle into a Dogfish Head pint glass.

Appearance: Pours hazy with a couple fingers of frothy, off-white head. The head lingers for a long time and leaves a good amount of lacing.

Aroma: Has a classic Noble hop, pilsner aroma.

Mouthfeel: Initially has a strong carbonation bite that goes away after time and becomes more drinkable. Medium mouthfeel that leaves a cloying stickiness causing a lot of mouthwatering after every sip.

Taste: A crisp, clean pilsner flavor. Hoppy for a pilsner, even an imperial pilsner. More hop bitterness than hop flavor. A minor, malty sweetness. Slight alcohol in the finish.

Conclusion

My Antonia is another great offering from Dogfish Head. Compared to their other rarities and extreme beers it’s certainly tame, but is a good contrast for them. My Antonia falls right in line with the pilsner kick I’ve been on recently. Having said that, it’s not my favorite imperial pilsner, that’s currently reserved for Sweetwater Brewing Company’s Magnum IP. As of this review, My Antonia isn’t available in Georgia and I grabbed this bottle on a recent trip to Florida. I’ll certainly pick up another bottle when given the opportunity. Why Florida get’s this and Georgie doesn’t is beyond me…

Food pairings: Spicy Asian food.

Cellar-able: No, being a pilsner, this would not age well.

Comparable beers: Sweetwater Magnum IP and Sam Adams Hallertau Imperial Pilsner are two I’ve had.
